Sidebar & information architecture

Today the sidebar carries 15 destinations nested under 4 collapsible groups. Several of those groups are really one job split across thin pages. The proposal flattens the rail and consolidates related features into hub pages with in-page tabs — fewer clicks, less scanning, one place per job. Everything stays role-aware.

The problem
1

Two clicks to anywhere

Every page lives inside a collapsible group, so reaching it means expand-then-click. An admin juggles 15 leaves across 4 accordions.

2

Thin pages, fragmented jobs

HR is 3 pages (Time / Leave / Check-in) for one self-service job. Admin fans out to 5 separate surfaces that are visited together.

3

Half-applied hub pattern

Development already has an “Overview” hub — yet Academy, Reading and Gym are also listed as siblings. The rail duplicates what the hub already aggregates.

Why this is the right call
  • Flatten the rail to single-level destinations. A worker sees 5 items, a lead/admin 6 — no accordions to expand. The rail becomes a map, not a filing cabinet.
  • One page per job, tabs for the facets. Growth = Overview · Academy · Reading · Gym. Time & Leave = Time · Leave · Check-in. Admin = People · Reports · Payroll · Manage · Settings. This is the exact “many features in one page” you asked for.
  • Deep-links & muscle memory survive. Each tab keeps its own URL (/growth?tab=academy), so notifications, bookmarks and the command palette still land on the right facet — the page just no longer needs its own rail slot.
  • Role-gating moves onto tabs. A tab you can’t use simply isn’t rendered; the hub still shows. Payroll/Manage stay super-admin, Settings admin+ — same rules, fewer top-level entries.
  • North Star stays first-class. It’s culture, not a feature — kept as its own destination on purpose, not buried in a hub.
Scope note: this is a navigation/IA change — the pages themselves already exist. The work is (a) a flat NAV with a tabs concept, (b) a shared hub-tab strip component, (c) redirects from old routes to ?tab= so nothing breaks. Task Board stays its own destination (it’s heavy and used constantly).
